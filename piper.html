The Piper at the Gates of Dawn

This was made under the mentorship of Markus Wreland, who was very kind enough to take part in Capilano University’s mentorship program. Together, we created a brief that entailed creating an animated album cover that could help connect to newer and younger fans of the classic band Pink Floyd. The cover had to read well on social media, loop seamlessly to attract more views, and finally, connect with the band’s visual identity in some way.

Throughout the project I learned to embrace change and challenges, and after lots of exploratory work with Markus Wreland, the concept of “dawn” emerged – since the album is the first of Pink Floyd’s long and legendary discography, I wanted to capture the feeling of bursting forth into adventure and possibility, while linking back to the rest of the band’s discography. The glass material used in the 3D animation is intended to be a subtle reference to Pink Floyd’s most famous album, the Dark Side of the Moon, which also depicts a glass prism reflecting light. The animated album cover is a looping animation of a small glass ball exploding into lots of glass shards reflecting violet and pink lights, revealing a hoof print – a nod to the piper mentioned in the album’s title.

I would like to extend a very special thanks to Markus Wreland for guiding and encouraging me on this project, especially during the early phases!
