Process Notes: The Beginning

Blogging about things I learn during experiments in 3D.

Social media platforms rise and fall, but I suppose websites are almost and hopefully forever. In this blog I will be writing sporadically about things I discover while experimenting with my work. Over here, there’s no metrics or fads that I have to measure myself up to, and much more space to detail insights I get from my work. I’m writing mostly for myself, to track how much I’ve learned from studying on my own and to track my understanding of 3D graphics.

Much of my experiments on the extras page is the result of me setting out to achieve a certain look, and much of this blog will go in depth into describing what will be going on in there.

A lot of shader node maps and technical jargon will be seen on this page, but I’ll try to explain myself as clearly as I can. I can’t claim to have the expertise of computer scientists, but I’ll try my best!

For now, here’s a node graph showcasing how I achieved the appearance of shiny, shattered glass in one of my portfolio pieces – a sneak peek of future blog posts.
